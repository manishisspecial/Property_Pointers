Tips
How to Control Construction Costs
📐 Plan thoroughly first
Changes after construction starts cost 3-5x more. Finalise all plans before breaking ground.
🏗️ Use local materials
Locally sourced bricks, sand and aggregates can reduce material costs by 15-20%.
📊 Get 3 contractor quotes
Always compare at least 3 contractors. Price variation can be 25-30% for same scope.
💧 Phase construction
Build ground floor first, use it, then add upper floors. Reduces loan burden.
🔍 Monitor steel prices
Steel is 20-25% of total cost. Time purchases when prices dip.
📋 Get RERA approval
Required for any construction. Helps with future resale and legal protection.
FAQ
Construction Cost FAQ

Construction cost in India ranges from ₹1,200-1,600 per sqft for basic quality, ₹1,800-2,800 for standard, and ₹2,800-4,500 per sqft for premium quality construction as of 2026. Mumbai is the most expensive, Jaipur and Tier 3 cities the most affordable.

A 1000 sqft house costs approximately ₹14-18 lakh for basic quality, ₹20-28 lakh for standard quality, and ₹30-50 lakh for premium quality in 2026. This excludes land cost, approval fees and interior furniture.

The biggest factors: 1) Material quality (especially tiles, sanitary ware, fittings — premium vs basic is 2-3x difference). 2) Structural design (more columns, beams, or complex architecture). 3) City location (Mumbai is 25% more than Noida). 4) Labour quality and supervision.

Building a house is generally 20-30% cheaper per sqft than buying a ready apartment in the same area — but requires land ownership, contractor management, approval processes and takes 12-24 months. Apartments offer ready possession, amenities and RERA protection.

You need: Building Plan Approval from local authority (Municipal Corporation / DUDA), RERA registration if selling, NOC from fire department for buildings above 15m, environmental clearance for large projects. Costs vary by municipality — budget 2-5% of construction cost for approvals.
