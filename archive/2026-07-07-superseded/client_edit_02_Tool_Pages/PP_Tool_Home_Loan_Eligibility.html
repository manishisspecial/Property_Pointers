Loan Eligibility FAQ

On ₹50,000 take-home with no existing EMIs, you qualify for approximately ₹23-26 lakh at 8.5% for 20 years. Banks allow 40-50% of income as total EMI. Adding a working spouse increases this to ₹40-46 lakh combined.

SBI minimum is ₹10,000/month, HDFC ₹15,000, ICICI ₹20,000. Salaried employees need 6 months at current employer. Self-employed need 3 years of ITR. Government employees typically get better terms.

Yes — significantly. Adding a working spouse increases eligibility 40-60%. ₹50,000 individual income gives ₹23L eligibility. Add spouse earning ₹40,000 and combined ₹90,000 gives ₹41-46L. Co-applicant also gets separate tax benefits on EMI.

CIBIL 750+ gets best rates — often 0.25-0.5% lower than standard. On ₹50L for 20 years, 0.5% lower saves ₹3,600/month and ₹8.6L total interest. Check CIBIL for free at cibil.com before applying.

Yes. Self-employed need: 3 years stable ITR showing income, business continuity of 3+ years, 12-month bank statements, CIBIL 700+. Eligibility typically calculated at 3-4× annual net profit. Some banks use gross receipts.

Salaried: salary slips (3 months), Form 16, bank statements (6 months), Aadhaar, PAN, employment letter. Self-employed: ITR (3 years), P&L and balance sheet. Property: sale agreement, chain of title deeds, approved building plan.
