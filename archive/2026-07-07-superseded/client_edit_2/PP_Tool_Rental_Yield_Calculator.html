Rental Yield FAQ

Average residential yield is 2.5-4.5%. Hyderabad leads at 4-5.2%, Bangalore at 3.8-4.5%, Pune and Noida at 3.2-4%. Mumbai has lowest at 2.5-3.2% due to very high property prices relative to rent.

Yes — 3.5-5% gross yield is considered good for residential property. Add 10-14% annual appreciation and combined return reaches 13-19% annually — significantly better than FD at 6.5-7.5%.

Hyderabad HITEC City — 4-5.2% gross yield. Followed by Bangalore IT corridor at 3.8-4.5% and Pune Hinjewadi at 3.5-4.5%. IT workforce expansion is the primary demand driver in all three.

Gross Yield = (Monthly Rent × 12 ÷ Property Value) × 100. Net Yield deducts annual maintenance, property tax and insurance. For ₹60L flat at ₹20,000/month: (2,40,000 ÷ 60,00,000) × 100 = 4% gross yield.

Commercial yields 6-10% vs 2.5-4.5% residential. But commercial requires ₹50L+ investment, longer vacancy periods (3-6 months), complex TDS compliance and management. Residential is more reliable, liquid and easier to manage.

1 month vacancy reduces effective annual yield by ~8.3%. On a 4% yield property, that becomes 3.67% effective yield. Furnished properties in IT corridors near offices have shortest vacancy — often under 2 weeks.
