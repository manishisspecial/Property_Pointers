FAQ
Rent vs Buy FAQ

Buying makes sense if: you plan to stay 5+ years, have 20% down payment ready, EMI is under 45% of income, and the property is in a growing market (Hyderabad, Noida, Bangalore). Renting is better if: staying less than 3 years, city has high price-to-rent ratio (Mumbai 28-35x), or you need capital flexibility.

A price-to-annual-rent ratio below 20x means buying is better value. Hyderabad and Jaipur are at 18-22x — buying makes sense. Mumbai is 28-35x — renting gives better value. Noida, Bangalore and Gurugram are at 20-26x depending on the micro-market.

In cities with 10-14% annual appreciation (Noida, Hyderabad, Bangalore IT corridors), buying typically becomes financially superior after 3-5 years. In Mumbai where price-to-rent ratios are high, the break-even is 7-10 years. Our calculator shows the exact crossover point for your inputs.

This depends on investment returns vs property appreciation. If your down payment invested in equity/FD earns 8-10% and the property appreciates 12-15% annually, buying wins significantly. But if property appreciation is only 5-6% (overvalued markets), investing the down payment can be better.

Buying costs beyond the purchase price: stamp duty (5-8%), registration (1%), home loan processing fee (0.5-1%), property tax (0.1-0.5% p.a.), society maintenance (₹2,000-10,000/month), insurance, repair and renovation. Our calculator accounts for these in the total cost analysis.
