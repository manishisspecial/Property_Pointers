Complete Guide

7 Steps to YourFirst Home.

Click any step to expand. Complete each step before moving to the next.

01
🔍 Research & Understand
Before anything else — understand your market
›

Most first-time buyers make the mistake of visiting sites before doing research. Start here.

Checklist for this step
  • ✓Understand the difference between Super Area and Carpet Area (builders inflate by 20-40%)
  • ✓Learn what RERA is and why it matters for every project you consider
  • ✓Read about builder delivery track records — not just ratings
  • ✓Understand which city matches your budget and lifestyle goals
Key things to know
What is your actual budget?

Do not just think about the property price. Add: 6-8% stamp duty + registration, 3-6 months EMI before possession, interior cost (Rs 200-600/sqft for a basic setup). Many first-time buyers are shocked by these extras.

New launch vs Ready to Move?

Ready-to-move: no RERA risk, no delay risk, can see before you buy. New launch: cheaper price, but 2-4 year wait, builder risk. For your first home — Ready-to-Move is almost always safer.

02
💰 Fix Your Budget
Know exactly what you can afford
›

Your budget is not just the property price. There are 5 numbers you need to know.

Checklist for this step
  • ✓Calculate your EMI and check if it is under 40% of take-home salary
  • ✓Check your home loan eligibility from banks before looking at properties
  • ✓Run the affordability calculator to find your real maximum budget
  • ✓Check current home loan rates across 15 banks
Key things to know
The 40% rule

Your total EMI (home loan + any other loans) should not exceed 40% of your take-home salary. Banks call this FOIR — Fixed Obligation to Income Ratio.

Hidden costs to budget for

Stamp duty (5-7% of property price) + Registration (1%) + GST on under-construction (5%) + Society charges + Car parking (Rs 3-10L separately) + Interior setup

03
🏗️ Research Builders
Not all builders are equal
›

The builder you choose matters more than the project. Check their track record before anything else.

Checklist for this step
  • ✓Check builder Trust Score (RERA + delivery track record)
  • ✓Check possession countdown for their current projects
  • ✓Read verified buyer reviews from past buyers
  • ✓Compare up to 3 builders side by side
Key things to know
The one question to ask

How many projects did this builder complete on time in the last 10 years? Ask them directly. Check our delivery tracker. A builder with 80%+ on-time delivery is rare and valuable.

Red flags to watch

Self-declared RERA compliance (always verify at .gov.in), no past buyer reviews, promised possession dates that keep changing, aggressive sales tactics to sign quickly.

04
📍 Shortlist & Visit
Smart site visits
›

Visit at least 3 projects before deciding. Know what to check when you are there.

Checklist for this step
  • ✓Use Property Report Card to grade each property before visiting
  • ✓See which locality has best appreciation and connectivity
  • ✓Check Circle Rate vs Market Rate for the locality
  • ✓Run ROI Calculator for investment potential
Key things to know
What to check at site visit

Actual carpet area (measure yourself). Natural light and ventilation. Floor, view, parking position. Construction quality (check columns, ceiling height). Check if promised amenities are actually built.

Always bring a checklist

Floor plan copy, RERA number to verify on-site, builder brochure to compare promises vs reality, list of questions about completion timeline and OC status.

05
🤝 Negotiate
Get the best price
›

Every property has negotiation room. Know how much before you start.

Checklist for this step
  • ✓Use Negotiation Edge tool for your city and property
  • ✓Run Should I Buy Now? to check market timing
  • ✓Check Stamp Duty before finalising to avoid surprises
  • ✓Ask AI Advisor for negotiation tips in Hindi or English
Key things to know
Average negotiation room in India

Ready-to-move: 5-8% off listed price is realistic. Under-construction new launch: 3-5% off, but more room on freebies (car parking, floor rise, PLC charges). Commercial: 8-15% room.

Best negotiating position

Tell the builder you are comparing 3 projects. Show them your loan pre-approval (proves you are serious). Offer faster payment schedule in exchange for a discount.

06
📋 Legal & Loan
The most important step
›

Do not skip legal due diligence. This is where most first-time buyers make expensive mistakes.

Checklist for this step
  • ✓Verify RERA registration and check for any orders or penalties
  • ✓Know your full RERA rights as a buyer
  • ✓Check Stamp Duty for your state
  • ✓Calculate total loan cost including all fees
Key things to know
Hire a property lawyer

For Rs 10,000-25,000, a property lawyer will review your sale agreement and identify any problematic clauses. Worth every rupee on a property worth crores.

Key things to check in sale agreement

Possession date (with builder penalty clause), carpet area guarantee, RERA number in agreement, all promised amenities listed, payment schedule matches your loan disbursement.

07
🎉 Register & Move In
Final steps
›

You are almost there. Here is what happens from booking to possession.

Checklist for this step
  • ✓Calculate Registration charges and plan accordingly
  • ✓Join PropertyPointers community to connect with other buyers in same project
  • ✓Share your Ghar Milaya story and help other first-time buyers
  • ✓Rate your builder and advisor — help the next buyer
Key things to know
Timeline from booking to possession

Under-construction: Booking → Agreement (30-60 days) → Construction period (2-4 years) → OC → Possession offer → Registration. Ready-to-move: Booking → Agreement (30-60 days) → Possession offer → Registration.

After possession checklist

Possession letter, OC copy from builder, all original documents, society share certificate, first maintenance receipt, Completion Certificate from local authority.
